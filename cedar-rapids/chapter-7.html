What Is Chapter 7 Bankruptcy?

Chapter 7, sometimes called "liquidation bankruptcy," eliminates most unsecured debts - credit cards, medical bills, personal loans - in exchange for surrendering non-exempt assets. In practice, most Chapter 7 cases are "no-asset" cases, meaning filers keep everything they own.

Cedar Rapids residents file Chapter 7 through the Northern District of Iowa (N.D. Iowa) at 111 7th Ave SE, Cedar Rapids, IA 52401.

Means Test: Do You Qualify?

The means test compares your income to Iowa's median income. If you are below the median for your household size, you automatically qualify.

If your income exceeds the median, you may still qualify after deducting allowed expenses. If not, Chapter 13 is an alternative that does not require a means test.

Chapter 7 Timeline in Cedar Rapids

1Credit counseling - Complete an approved course within 180 days before filing.

2File petition - Submit forms to the Northern District of Iowa (N.D. Iowa). The automatic stay takes effect immediately, stopping collections, garnishments, and lawsuits.

3341 meeting - Approximately 30 days after filing, you attend a brief meeting with the trustee. Creditors may attend but rarely do.

4Debtor education - Complete a second financial management course before discharge.

5Discharge - Approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting, the court enters your discharge order eliminating qualifying debts.

Iowa Exemptions in Chapter 7

Iowa exemptions: UNLIMITED homestead (must be less than 1/2 acre in a city or 40 acres elsewhere), $7,000 vehicle. Iowa is one of the most debtor-friendly states in the country for homestead protection.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I qualify for Chapter 7 in Cedar Rapids?

To qualify for Chapter 7 in Iowa, your household income must be below the state median or you must pass the means test calculation. For a single filer in Iowa, the median income threshold is approximately $55,724.

How long does Chapter 7 take in Cedar Rapids?

Chapter 7 typically takes 3-4 months from filing to discharge in the Northern District of Iowa (N.D. Iowa). The 341 meeting of creditors is usually scheduled about 30 days after filing.

Will I lose my car in Chapter 7?

Iowa allows you to exempt up to $7,000 in vehicle equity. If your car equity is within this limit, you can keep it. If you are still making payments, you can reaffirm the loan and keep the vehicle.

What debts does Chapter 7 eliminate?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debts including credit cards, medical bills, personal loans, and old utility bills. It does not eliminate student loans (in most cases), child support, alimony, most tax debts, or criminal fines.

How much does Chapter 7 cost in Cedar Rapids?

The court filing fee is $338. Attorney fees in Cedar Rapids typically range from $1,000 to $2,500. You must also complete two credit counseling courses at approximately $25-$50 total.
