Bankruptcy Basics in Cedar Rapids

If you are struggling with debt in Cedar Rapids, bankruptcy may give you a path to a fresh start. Most people file either Chapter 7 (liquidation) or Chapter 13 (repayment plan).

Cedar Rapids residents file through the Northern District of Iowa (N.D. Iowa). The courthouse is located at 111 7th Ave SE, Cedar Rapids, IA 52401.

Iowa exemptions: UNLIMITED homestead (must be less than 1/2 acre in a city or 40 acres elsewhere), $7,000 vehicle. Iowa is one of the most debtor-friendly states in the country for homestead protection.

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13 in Cedar Rapids

Chapter 7

  • Eliminates most unsecured debt
  • Takes 3-4 months
  • Must pass the means test
  • May lose non-exempt property
  • Filing fee: $338

Chapter 13

  • Repay debts over 3-5 years
  • Keep all your property
  • No means test required
  • Can catch up on mortgage/car payments
  • Filing fee: $313

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I file for bankruptcy in Cedar Rapids?

You file for bankruptcy in Cedar Rapids through the Northern District of Iowa (N.D. Iowa). The courthouse is located at 111 7th Ave SE, Cedar Rapids, IA 52401. You will need to complete credit counseling, gather financial documents, and file a petition with the court. Most people hire an attorney, though you can file pro se.

What bankruptcy exemptions apply in Cedar Rapids?

Iowa exemptions: UNLIMITED homestead (must be less than 1/2 acre in a city or 40 acres elsewhere), $7,000 vehicle. Iowa is one of the most debtor-friendly states in the country for homestead protection.

How much does bankruptcy cost in Cedar Rapids?

Chapter 7 filing fees are $338 and Chapter 13 filing fees are $313. Attorney fees in Cedar Rapids typically range from $1,000 to $2,500 for Chapter 7 and $3,000 to $5,000 for Chapter 13.

Should I file Chapter 7 or Chapter 13 in Cedar Rapids?

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debt in 3-4 months but requires passing a means test. Chapter 13 lets you keep property while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Your income, assets, and goals determine which is best.

Can I keep my house if I file bankruptcy in Cedar Rapids?

Iowa provides a Unlimited homestead exemption. If your equity is within that limit, you can keep your home in Chapter 7. In Chapter 13, you can keep your home while catching up on missed payments.
