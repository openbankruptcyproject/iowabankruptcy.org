Understanding Exemptions

Bankruptcy exemptions are the legal protections that let you keep property when you file. Iowa exemptions: UNLIMITED homestead (must be less than 1/2 acre in a city or 40 acres elsewhere), $7,000 vehicle. Iowa is one of the most debtor-friendly states in the country for homestead protection.

In Chapter 7, exemptions determine what a trustee can sell to pay creditors. In Chapter 13, exemptions affect how much you must pay unsecured creditors through your plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the homestead exemption in Iowa?

Iowa provides a Unlimited homestead exemption that protects equity in your primary residence. If your home equity is within this amount, you can keep your home when filing Chapter 7 bankruptcy.

Can I keep my car filing bankruptcy in Cedar Rapids?

Iowa allows you to exempt up to $7,000 in vehicle equity. If you owe more than your car is worth or your equity is within the exemption, you keep the car.

Does Iowa allow federal bankruptcy exemptions?

Iowa allows filers to choose between state and federal exemptions, though state exemptions are typically more favorable.

What is the wildcard exemption in Iowa?

Iowa provides a $1,000 wildcard exemption that can be applied to any property of your choosing. This is useful for protecting assets that do not fit into other exemption categories.
