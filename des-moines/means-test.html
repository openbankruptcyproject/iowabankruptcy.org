Des Moines Means Test: Do You Qualify for Chapter 7?

The means test determines whether you can file Chapter 7 bankruptcy in Des Moines. Your income is compared to Iowa's median income for your household size.

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

1Calculate your current monthly income (CMI) -- Add all income from all sources for the six months before filing. Divide by six, then multiply by 12 to annualize.

2Determine your household size -- Count everyone who lives in your household and depends on your income.

3Compare to median -- If your annualized CMI is at or below the median for your household size, you qualify for Chapter 7 automatically.

4If above median -- Deduct IRS-approved expenses, secured debt payments, and priority debts. If your remaining disposable income over 60 months is less than $9,075, you still qualify.

What If You Do Not Qualify?

  • File Chapter 13 instead -- no means test required
  • Wait and refile -- the test uses the six months before filing, so a drop in income can change the result
  • Challenge with special circumstances -- medical conditions, job loss, or other factors
  • Re-examine deductions -- health insurance, childcare, education, retirement contributions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the bankruptcy means test?

The means test compares your income to the median income in your state. If your income is below the median for your household size, you automatically qualify for Chapter 7. If above, you must pass a second calculation factoring in allowed expenses.

What is the median income for the means test in Iowa in 2026?

The 2026 Iowa median income thresholds are: 1-person household $55,724, 2-person $72,116, 3-person $85,880, 4-person $104,316. Add approximately $9,900 for each additional person.

What happens if I fail the means test?

You can file Chapter 13 instead (no means test), wait until your income drops, challenge the presumption of abuse with special circumstances, or re-examine your allowable deductions.

Does the means test apply to Chapter 13?

No. However, your income level determines whether your Chapter 13 plan lasts 3 years (below median) or 5 years (above median).
