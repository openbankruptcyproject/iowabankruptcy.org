What Is Chapter 13 Bankruptcy?

Chapter 13 bankruptcy -- often called the "wage earner's plan" -- lets you reorganize your debts into a manageable repayment plan. Unlike Chapter 7, you keep all of your property while making payments over 3-5 years.

Chapter 13 is particularly useful if you are behind on mortgage payments, need to save a home from foreclosure, or have income that exceeds the Chapter 7 means test threshold.

How Chapter 13 Works in Des Moines

1Credit counseling -- Complete an approved course before filing.

2File petition and plan -- Submit your petition and proposed repayment plan to the Southern District of Iowa (S.D. Iowa). The automatic stay takes effect immediately.

3Begin payments -- You must begin making plan payments within 30 days of filing, even before the plan is confirmed.

4341 meeting -- Meet briefly with the trustee approximately 30 days after filing.

5Confirmation hearing -- The court reviews and approves your plan, typically 30-45 days after the 341 meeting.

6Complete plan payments -- Make all payments over 3-5 years.

7Discharge -- After completing all payments, remaining qualifying unsecured debts are discharged.

Chapter 7 vs. Chapter 13

Chapter 7

  • Eliminates debt in 3-4 months
  • Must pass means test
  • May lose non-exempt property
  • Cannot catch up on mortgage arrears

Chapter 13

  • 3-5 year repayment plan
  • No means test
  • Keep all property
  • Can cure mortgage defaults

Frequently Asked Questions

How does Chapter 13 work in Des Moines?

Chapter 13 lets you reorganize your debts into a 3-5 year repayment plan. You make monthly payments to a trustee who distributes funds to creditors. At the end of the plan, remaining qualifying debts are discharged.

How long is a Chapter 13 plan?

If your income is below the state median, your plan can be as short as 3 years. If above the median, the plan must be 5 years. The maximum is 5 years regardless of income.

Can I keep my house in Chapter 13?

Yes. Chapter 13 is specifically designed to help you keep your home. You can catch up on missed mortgage payments through your plan while maintaining current payments. Iowa's Unlimited homestead exemption provides additional protection.

How much does Chapter 13 cost?

The court filing fee is $313. Attorney fees in Des Moines typically range from $3,000 to $5,000 and are usually paid through the plan itself, so you do not need the full amount up front.

What debts must I repay in Chapter 13?

Priority debts (taxes, child support) must be paid in full. Secured debts (mortgage, car loans) must be kept current. Unsecured creditors receive a percentage based on your disposable income -- sometimes as low as 0%.
